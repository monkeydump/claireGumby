Hello,

I am a certified SEO copywriter, content marketer and owner of The Content Boutique.

If the terms SEO (search engine optimisation) copywriting and content marketing sound technical and a little bit scary, I can assure you they are not. They are marketing communication strategies that make sense.

In fact I am also a classically trained (Chartered Institute of Marketing) marketer. Underpinning my work is a sound knowledge and experience of marketing strategy.

You will be pleased to hear that the foundations of classic marketing strategy have not changed. Content marketers still focus on:

  • Understanding your target market (for B2B marketing the decision making unit).
  • Understanding your competition.
  • Understanding your buy cycle.

What has changed are the tools that can be used to support your marketing communications strategy.

Old school marketing

My first website copywriting project (or should I say corporate brochure online) went live back in the late 1990s when the terms SEO copywriting and content marketing were unheard of.

At the start of my career I spent a lot of time writing in house for various companies that variously described themselves as:

  • 'Leaders in their field.'
  • Working 'in partnership with their clients.'
  • Providing 'cutting edge services.'

However it always troubled me that these phrases were so overused. In each sector I worked in, how could there be so many leaders in their fields? In the words of Highlander: 'there can be only one'.

From marketing to content marketing

Technology and marketing has moved on. Today your business can take advantage of a better way to differentiate and stand out from the crowd.

This is why I am excited about the concept of content marketing. According to The Content Marketing Institute it is:

'The process of creating and distributing relevant content to attract, acquire and engage a clearly defined and understood targeted audience.'

Content marketing turns traditional marketing on its head. Instead of sending push messages, a content marketer communicates valuable information geared to solving your client's problems, thus building trust and loyalty that ultimately rewards you in new business.
